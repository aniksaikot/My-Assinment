In July 2024, Bangladesh witnessed a historic student movement against inequality and government corruption. Triggered by police brutality and unemployment, protests spread nationwide, demanding reform and Sheikh Hasina's resignation. Violence escalated after a student was killed in Rangpur, intensifying demonstrations. Hasina resigned in August, replaced by an interim government led by Dr. Yunus. The movement, driven by youth leadership, marked a turning point, restoring democracy and empowering Bangladesh's new generation.
